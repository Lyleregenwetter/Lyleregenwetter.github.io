About Me

I'm Lyle, a proud midwesterner, dedicated mountain lover, and aspiring foodie (quite the incongruous combination, I know). I grew up in Champaign, Illinois, home of the University of Illinois, where my interests have evolved from math competitions in my youth to robotics in my high school years to data-driven design and AI during my undergraduate studies at UIUC. I now reside in Cambridge, MA, where I am a Ph.D. Candidate at MIT in Professor Faez Ahmed's DeCoDE Lab. I am very excited about the intersection of AI and design and am thrilled to be a part of the growing data-driven design research community.

Academic Bio

Lyle Regenwetter is a Ph.D. Candidate at MIT. He received his S.M. in Mechanical Engineering from MIT in 2022, as well as a B.S. in Electrical Engineering and a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering with highest honors from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2020. Lyle has received numerous academic distinctions, such as the Chancellor's scholarship, Association for Facilities Engineering Award, and Kenneth J. Trigger award at UIUC and the Morningside Academy of Design Fellowship at MIT.
